Journal of Urban Affairs · 2026
Why it's interesting: New large (>50-unit) market-rate buildings affect nearby rents, but the effect varies by the quality and submarket of existing housing — nuancing the claim that new supply uniformly lowers local rents.
Characteristics of a Sufficient Statistic to Measure City Housing Prices
Broxterman, Larson & Yezer · NBER WP 35144 · Apr 2026
Why it's interesting: Common repeat-sales and hedonic indexes (Case-Shiller, FHFA) aren't quantity-weighted and can misstate a city's price level and change — implying widely-used benchmarks may be biased, and showing how to fix them.

Home Mortgage Disclosure Act (HMDA) Data
CFPB / FFIEC · updated annually · canonical
What's in it: The most comprehensive public record of US mortgage applications and originations — lender, loan terms, geography, and borrower demographics. The standard source for housing-finance, credit-access, and fair-lending analysis.
Survey of Construction / Characteristics of New Housing (SOC)
U.S. Census Bureau · annual · canonical
What's in it: Annual detail on what new homes actually are — size, lot size, sale price, and features — the standard series for trends in new single-family and multifamily characteristics.
A Picture of Subsidized Households
HUD · annual · canonical (government-maintained)
What's in it: Program-level data on federally subsidized housing — units, tenants, and rents down to the development and tract level — the reference dataset for the assisted-housing stock.
