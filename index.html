★ Paper of the Week · Published May 25, 2026
Unlocking Mortgage Lock-In: Equilibrium Effects in a Spatial Housing Ladder Model
Julia Fonseca, Lu Liu & Pierre Mabille · NBER Working Paper 35237
Why it's interesting: Lock-in is usually told as a supply story, but this paper shows it raises net housing demand — "missing downsizers" stay in larger homes, especially in expensive areas, offsetting a chunk of the price decline higher rates would otherwise cause. It lifts prices ~4.4% and rents ~1.5% versus a no-lock-in world. The sharpest takeaway: demand-side responses to lock-in are estimated at roughly $650,000 per marginal move, so they're poorly targeted.
Citation signal: brand-new working paper, so counts aren't yet meaningful — selected on result strength and timeliness. It was the only quality paper inside the 7-day window.

Zoning & Land-Use Reform (highest-interest)

How Big Upzonings Affect Housing Supply
Urban Institute · Apr 2026
Seven NYC neighborhood-scale upzonings produced 4,000+ extra units within four years versus comparable non-upzoned parcels — rare causal, quantified evidence that big upzonings actually move supply on a meaningful timescale.
Zoning and the Dynamics of Urban Redevelopment
Vincent Rollet · SSRN working paper · Oct 2025
Dynamic GE model on NYC parcel data: zoning strongly constrains growth, but relaxing it takes decades to show up and does little in cheap or already-dense areas — tempers "upzone and supply follows" optimism.

America's Rental Housing 2026

Rents dipping and vacancies up, yet cost-burdened renters at a record high — a cooling market that still isn't easing affordability.

Construction

Why Has Construction Productivity Stagnated? The Role of Land-Use RegulationFlagged: just outside window
D'Amico, Glaeser, Gyourko, Kerr & Ponzetto · NBER WP 33188 · Nov 2024

Citation signal: landmark, heavily cited and widely covered (Cato, ITIF); pinned per standing preference despite Nov-2024 date.
Potential Pathways to Scale Innovative Construction Methods in California
UC Berkeley Terner Center · Mar 2026
Based on 65+ interviews: modular/industrialized construction keeps failing to scale because of financing, code fragmentation, and pipeline volatility — not technology.

By-Right Development & Permitting

AEI State Housing Supply Legislative Update
AEI Housing Center · 2026
Tracks 21 state legislatures and projects the 2026 Starter Homes / Small Lot wave could add ~172,700 homes/year; companion piece argues zoning, not capital cost, is the binding constraint.
Built Out Cities? A New Approach to Measuring Land Use Regulation
Monkkonen, Manville & Lens (UCLA) · Journal of Housing Economics · 2024
Uses cities' self-reported zoned capacity rather than regulatory indexes; finds modest deregulation in high-demand cities yields far more housing than big deregulation in low-demand ones — a methodological advance for permitting analysis.

Federal Reserve Bank of Philadelphia · Economic Insights Q3 2025
Each point of lock-in cuts a homeowner's probability of selling ~18% and listings 21–23%, roughly doubling time on market and pushing prices up under tight conditions — the empirical complement to this week's featured paper.

~¼ of single-family homes now built as build-to-rent; multifamily starts falling fastest since 2021 — a structural shift in what gets built and who owns it.

Housing Datasets

A mix of canonical and niche datasets relevant to supply, permitting, zoning, prices/rents, and density.

National Zoning Atlas
Cornell / National Zoning Atlas team · ongoing
Granular dataset extracting 200+ regulatory characteristics per zoning district across ~3,000 jurisdictions — the leading tool for analyzing by-right permitting and land-use rules across places. Provenance: academic-led, actively maintained.
Wharton Residential Land Use Regulatory Index (WRLURI)
Joseph Gyourko et al. · Wharton / NBER WP 26573 · 2019
The standard survey-based index of local regulatory stringency across 2,600+ communities — foundational benchmark for cross-jurisdiction permitting/regulation research. Provenance: Wharton, peer-reviewed.
50-State Property Tax Comparison Study
Lincoln Institute of Land Policy & Minnesota Center for Fiscal Excellence · 2025
Definitive cross-city property-tax benchmark (100+ cities, 4 property types) explaining why effective rates vary so widely — a reference dataset for modeling housing carrying costs across markets. Provenance: Lincoln Institute, established annual series.
